What is Occupational Therapy?

Occupational therapy is a holistic therapeutic practice that encompasses patients across the lifespan to do their occupations. Occupations can be described as anyhing that occupies your time, from self-care skills to leisure activities that are meaningful to the client.

Occupations:

ADLs: activities of daily living (toileting, hyginem bathing, mobility, feeding, sex, etc.) these DO NOT require a high level of inellect and are considered more basic.

IADL: instrumental activities of daily living (cooking, shopping, parenting, pet care, etc.) these DO require intellectual competence and planning/preperation.

Health management: maintaining ones health, healthdevices, appointments, medicine.

Work: jobs, volunteering, manual labor

Play: engaging in play, suspension of reality

Education: formal (school) or informal (learning a new skill like how to play piano, etc.)

Rest/Speel: rest, sleeping, sleep preparation

Social Participation: interacting with family, peers, friends, relationships

Is Occupational Therapy for me?

Occupational therapy is a very rewarding career that allows you to work with people that are in need of siecific therapy treatments to allow them to recover or regian functionality or much more. If you choose to go into occupatioal therapy, you will have a close and interactive relationship with your patients and you can watch them grow and regain their skills.

What do I need to go into Occupational Therapy?

To practice occupational therapy, you currently need to go to graduate school for occupatonal therapy for a masters degree or higher. However, in the next few years, the requirement to practice will be bumped up to require a doctorate degree.
